View on Official SXSW

Summary

SUUVI is a Cuban-Chinese composer, producer, and multidisciplinary artist whose roots in electronic cello-driven performance inform a bold, genre-fluid sonic vision. After 15+ years appearing internationally as a classical soloist in venues such as Carnegie Hall, La Fondation Louis Vuitton, and the Berlin Philharmonie - and touring as the solo cellist in Hans Zimmer’s live show - she expanded beyond the stage, blending cello, electronics, and storytelling to craft immersive works that reflect her multicultural background and social-impact mission. She has composed and produced original music for theater, dance, and film/TV, working across genres from contemporary classical to electronic, hip-hop, and pop, with artists like D-Smoke, Teyana Taylor, FAVE and many more - earning recognition for her boundary-pushing artistry and emotional depth. Her debut album, Dark Therapy, pushes post-classical music into avant-garde electronic and cinematic realms, inviting listeners into a space of introspection, healing, and sensory immersion.
