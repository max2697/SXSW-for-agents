Summary

La Texana, the music project of José Ramírez, was born in 2021 in Tijuana, Baja California. At 17, with just a laptop and guitar, he began recording music inspired by Spanish alternative rock and post-punk influences. He debuted with Nunca He Sabido Amar, and in 2023 released his first EP, Morro, featuring Un Día Como Hoy, now with over 6.5 million streams. In 2025, La Texana released his first full-length album, signed by the spanish label Sonido Muchacho "La Casa Que Cae", a 10-track record exploring identity and new beginnings, reaching over 8 million streams. It includes standout tracks like Terco, El Sol, and Dispara. In May, he performed a sold-out debut at Lunario of Auditorio Nacional and is now touring across Mexico (40+ cities) and 7 countries, with more dates to come. La Texana is currently preparing for his biggest show yet at Teatro Metropólitan in February 2026. His style continues to evolve, blending vulnerability and nostalgia, while connecting with a new generation of Mexican musicians and growing an international audience.
