Summary

Alina Pash is a Ukrainian artist now based in Los Angeles, building a bold new live experience in the U.S.—a mystic rap-based opera that fuses deep-root Ukrainian ritual, ethnic vocal power, and modern bass/electronic production. Her performances feel like ceremony: cinematic, high-voltage, and emotionally precise—where folklore meets the club, and tradition becomes future music. Back home, Alina built a major name fast: she performed on Kyiv’s main square for Independence Day in front of President Volodymyr Zelensky, and later that same night headlined the country’s biggest rave, Brave! Factory—proof of her rare ability to command both national stages and underground culture. She’s shared bills with top international acts, earned wide media attention, and continues to push Ukrainian sound into global pop and bass ecosystems. In March she drops a deluxe release with 15 remix features—largely U.S. collaborators—bridging her world into American scenes. Now, she’s developing a new album and an expanded 2026–2027 performance program—built for discovery rooms and tastemaker festivals in the U.S. and beyond.
